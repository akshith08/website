Recent Advances on IoT-assisted Wearable Sensor Systems for Healthcare Monitoring.

MDPI BIOSENSOR JOURNAL
  • IoT has played an essential role in many industries over the last few decades.
  • Recent advancements in the healthcare industry have made it possible to make healthcare accessible to more people and improve their overall health.
  • The next step in healthcare is to integrate it with IoT-assisted Wearable Sensor Systems seamlessly.

Recent Projects

MARTIAN ROVER PROTOTYPE
This is a mars prototype rover designed to traverse rough terrain and perform various assistive tasks. The chassis and the suspension is designed after various simulations. The rover has a 4-wheel drive with encoder feedback. The robotic arm was a 6DoF design with LRL configuration with a differential End-effector. The arm was designed with planetary gear motors and gear systems to make controlling intuitive. The Motors are controlled using Cytron Motor drivers and STM32F411E discovery board. I2C communication is established with Jetson Nano and is wirelessly controlled with a joystick. The arm is automated completely using cameras, without a line of sight. The control system is closed looped and the feedback is designed with minimal delay. The rover also works autonomously with the use of digicam and sensor array for obstacle avoidance.
SOIL COLLECTION AND ANALYSIS SYSTEM
This is an in-situ soil analysis system that is designed to be an attachment to any terrestrial rover model. It collects soil from the top layer and distributes it to various slots for chemical testing, which is completely automated. The chemical tests are monitored using cameras and other sensors to record the change in parameters and derive a conclusion on the result. We use stepper motors, linear actuators, BLDC and DC motors to actuate the system. We also have a large array of sensors to monitor the atmosphere and a self-designed anemometer to calculate the wind speed.
Techniques used:
  • Sensors
  • Power distribution system
  • Embedded Systems
PROSTHETIC ARM
The project involved designing a right arm from elbow down. The arm was assembled with 3D printed parts and micro-DC motors were used to actuate the fingers. The PCBs designed were highly compact given the minimal space available. The power and battery system were tested and designed to have a long run time. The control system is a closed loop and the feedback system was used to record the movement of the arm. An array of sensors was used to monitor the muscle movement of the upper arm and anticipate the position of the arm and fingers. The fingers were controlled using Arduino Nano Every, which was communicating with the master Raspberry Pi Zero through I2C.
Techniques used:
  • Power distribution board
  • Embedded system
